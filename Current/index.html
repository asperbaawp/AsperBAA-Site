About the business analtytics major

A major in Business Analytics will give you the understanding and tools necessary to work with and translate data into valuable outputs for any organization. This major will educate you on databases, tools and systems to process and evaluate data within an organizational context.

What is business analytics?

Business Analytics & Data.

Business analytics refers to taking in and processing business data and analyzing that data to identify trends, patterns, and root causes of business problems. These insights are used to make data-driven business decisions.

Organizations & Data

Nowadays, organizations rely on data to make decisions. Individuals who can work with data are becoming increasingly valuable to drive organizational decision making. The Business Analytics major will provide students with the knowledge and skillset to collect, analyze, and evaluate data in a variety of organizational contexts.

business analytics vs. mIS

Although there is overlap between Management Information Systems (MIS) and Business Analytics, Business Analytics focuses more heavily on databases and different tools used to collect, organize, analyze and evaluate data in a variety of organizational contexts.

What is the difference between business analytics and computer science?

Business analytics focuses on using computer technology to support a business’ operations, decision making, and planning by making sure that managers have the information they need to do their jobs. Computer science is more concerned with technology than management; it focuses on creating computer programs and improving computer performance.

Careers

Business analytics offers a dynamic and challenging career path. Typical business analytics jobs include:

  • Systems and Business Analysts
  • Database Designer and Administrator
  • Network Administrator
  • Website Manager
  • Web Developer
  • Internet Marketing Specialist
  • Customer Relationship Manager
  • Project Manager of Software Development

Marketing
